Empowering the Next Generation of Innovators

Kidpreneur is a global movement dedicated to nurturing entrepreneurial skills in children aged 8-16. Through our innovative programs, mentorship, and resources, we're creating a world where young minds can thrive as creative problem-solvers and future leaders.

Our Mission & Vision

We believe that every child has the potential to change the world. Our mission is to unlock that potential through entrepreneurship education.

Creating Future Leaders Through Entrepreneurship

At Kidpreneur, we're not just teaching business skills—we're fostering creativity, resilience, and ethical leadership. Our approach combines practical learning with mentorship to help young minds develop the mindset and skills needed to thrive in an ever-changing world.

We envision a future where young people are empowered to create solutions to real-world problems, where they approach challenges with confidence and creativity, and where they understand the power of innovation and collaboration.

Our Core Values

These principles guide everything we do at Kidpreneur and shape the experiences we create for young innovators.

Innovation

We encourage creative thinking and curiosity, empowering kids to develop unique solutions to real-world problems through design thinking and experimentation.

Empathy

We teach young entrepreneurs to understand and address real needs in their communities, fostering businesses that make a positive social impact.

Integrity

We emphasize ethical business practices, transparency, and social responsibility as foundations for sustainable entrepreneurship.

Our Journey

From a classroom project to a global movement—discover how Kidpreneur has evolved over the years.

How It All Began

Kidpreneur started in 2015 as a simple after-school program in a single classroom. Our founder, Sarah Johnson, noticed that traditional education wasn't adequately preparing students for the real-world challenges they would face as adults.

What began with just 12 students meeting twice a week has now grown into an international organization with programs in 42 countries. Along the way, we've learned, adapted, and expanded our vision while staying true to our core mission.

2015
Founded as an after-school program with 12 students in Portland, Oregon. Developed our first curriculum focused on lemonade stand economics.
2017
Expanded to 10 schools across Oregon. Launched our first summer entrepreneurship camp. Received recognition from the Department of Education.
2019
Went international with programs in Canada and Mexico. Developed our digital learning platform. Reached 1,000 young entrepreneurs.
2021
Pivoted to virtual programs during the pandemic, reaching 5,000+ students globally. Launched our mentorship matching platform.
2023
Reached 15,000+ young entrepreneurs across 42 countries. Launched our social impact fund for kid-led ventures. Recognized by UNESCO for educational innovation.

Our Impact

We measure success not just by businesses launched, but by lives transformed and communities strengthened.

Economic Impact

Our young entrepreneurs have launched over 3,500 micro-businesses, generating more than $2.3 million in revenue, with 30% of profits donated to community causes.

Educational Outcomes

92% of participants show improved problem-solving skills, 87% demonstrate increased confidence, and 78% show greater engagement in traditional academic subjects.

Community Engagement

Kidpreneur initiatives have addressed over 200 community challenges, from environmental issues to social justice, with solutions conceived and implemented by youth.

Global Reach

Our programs now operate in 42 countries, with materials translated into 15 languages, ensuring accessibility to diverse communities worldwide.

Sustainable Ventures

Over 60% of businesses started by our young entrepreneurs continue operating beyond the program, with 45% of founders starting additional ventures.

Recognition

Kidpreneur has received 18 awards for educational innovation and social impact, including recognition from the United Nations and World Economic Forum.

Our Programs
